#Motivation

In this post, I will review the book "The Worldly Philosophers - the lives, times, and ideas of the great economic thinkers -" by Robert L. Heilbroner. I bought this book a few years ago when I was in college. Professor Barreto, my favorite professor at DePauw University, used this book as a textbook in his History of Economic Thought class, which I did not get to take during my time at DePauw.

This series of posts will summarize the book and organize my thoughts and ideas on the historical development of economics and philosophy of political economy. I hope to read Manifesto of the Communist Party by Karl Marx and Frederick Engels (1848) and Capital - A Critique of Political Economy - Volume I by Karl Marx.
